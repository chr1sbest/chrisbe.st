Bio

Humble Beginnings

At the bottom of the rung, I was tasked with a monthly recurring 20+ hrs of manual data entry at an internship. Being crafty (lazy), I looked into alternative solutions and found a macro-scripting language. After studying up the docs for a few hours, I wrote a script to automate the task to complete the data entry in 10% of the time. As I kicked my feet up onto my desk and queued up some youtube videos while my script ran, I realized I had found my true love.

I managed to get my hands onto the unpublished internal API docs and improved my data entry script to run in less than 1% of the time and built a web interface using Python so that my team could make use of it. I (think) I had the curiosity, tenacity, and sharpness to be a software engineer, but I also knew that I lacked the fundamentals. I left the position to study computer science full-time with a rigorous plan and courseload with the hopes of landing an entry-level job after three months.

Coffee and Ramen

Armed with determination, boxes of Shin-Ramyun, and brilliant mentors, I worked through two of the most difficult courses Berkeley's CS program while building out a few toys of my own. After finishing up with my crash-course in three months, I landed a few job offers. The most important factor in choosing my first software job was "How much will I learn and how much will I be challenged in this position?" Of course, I picked an early-stage startup.

Drinking from the Firehose

As one of two engineers working on the project, I get to have a hand in just about every aspect of the application. Building our application is by far the most difficult thing I have ever done in my life. Fortunately, I also lucked out in having brilliant mentors, both on the tech and the professional side. They turned out to think that I was pretty cool too, and graciously offered me a co-founding partnership.

As frustrating and challenging software development can be, there are very few feelings better than mastering a language, library, skillset, etc., and being able to create something out of nothing.
